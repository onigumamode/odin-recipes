Okonomiyaki

Description

This gluten free okonomiyaki recipe makes a savory pancake dinner that has become a staple in our house. We often make a double batch to use up more cabbage and eggs - the leftovers keep well, and can be reheated on the stove top or in a toaster oven to keep them nice and crispy.

Makes 4 pancakes

Ingredients

  • ¼ head of small cabbage finely shredded
  • 2 thinly sliced spring onions
  • 4 shitake mushrooms thinly sliced (any mushrooms will work fine)
  • 1 portion of My Heart Beets 3 Ingredient Naan batter (1/2 cup almond flour, 1/2 cup tapioca flour, 1 cup coconut milk mixed together)
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • ½ tsp onion powder
  • ¼ cup water
  • pepper to taste
  • Optional - protein (we like to fry a bit of bacon right before we pour the batter into the pan, but shrimp or strips of tofu are also delicious!)
  • Mayo, aonori, and okonomiyaki sauce to taste for serving

Steps

  1. Wisk together the almond flour, tapioca flour, and coconut milk
  2. Add eggs one and a time, mixing between each addition
  3. Add the seasonings, spring onions, mushrooms, and finally the cabbage. Don't over add cabbage - the batter should still look wet and be able to cover the cabbage well
  4. Heat a swirl of oil in a pan or a griddle on medium heat. Fry the protein in the pan for a minute or so if using, then pour a ladle of batter over it/into the pan or griddle
  5. Fry until it is crispy and golden on one side, then flip and fry the other side until it is golden as well
  6. Serve with mayo, okonomiyaki sauce, and aonori to taste
